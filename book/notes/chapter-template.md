# Chapter planning template

## Working title

Name the engineering problem rather than the Bynk feature.

## Question

What difficult question should the reader be able to answer after this chapter?

## Opening pressure

Describe a recognisable system under pressure. Avoid beginning with terminology.

## Conventional response

What do capable teams usually do? Why is that response reasonable?

## Where information is lost

What important fact remains conventional, implicit, or enforced too late?

## Bynk's response

Which language idea retains that information? What can the compiler now know?

## Evidence

- successful program or fragment;
- rejected program and diagnostic;
- architecture or state diagram;
- effect on the evolving example system.

## Cost and counterargument

What flexibility is removed? Could disciplined TypeScript or another system
solve this adequately? Where does Bynk's answer not reach?

## Closing turn

How does this change the reader's understanding of the larger argument, and
what pressure leads naturally into the next chapter?

## Online sources to consult

Links only. Do not include documentation prose mechanically.

## What changes

The two editor scaffold catalogues — the LSP's `completion::SNIPPETS` and VS
Code's static `vscode-bynk/snippets/bynk.json` — had fallen behind the language:
between them they offered `context`/`commons`/`adapter`/`capability`/`provides`/
`service`/`agent`/`fn`/`type record`/`type enum` and a handful of handlers, but
**nothing** for the constructs the language grew since: the testing surface
(`suite`/`case`/`property`/`expect`), `actor`, agent `invariant`/`transition`,
function `requires`/`ensures` contracts, refined and `opaque` `type`s, and the
`uses`/`consumes`/`given` clauses. This slice adds those scaffolds, each
de-staled against the current grammar and proven by slice 0's compile guard.

This is a **tooling-only** slice: no grammar, checker, emitter, or spec change.
Migration is nil.

## Tooling delta

The four LSP responses ADR 0156 names, for the record: **completion** grows the
declaration/keyword-position snippet set (below); **hover**, **semantic tokens**,
and **signature help** are unchanged — a scaffold catalogue feeds only the
snippet-completion path.

- **`completion::SNIPPETS`** (was 5 entries) gains `commons`, `uses`, `consumes`,
  `type record`, `type enum`, `type refined`, `type opaque`, `fn`, `fn contract`
  (`requires`/`ensures`), `provides`, `actor`, `agent` (now carrying `invariant`
  and `transition`), `on http` (carrying a `given` clause), `on cron`, `suite`
  (with a `case`/`expect`), and `property` (with `for all`).
- **`bynk.json`** (was 11 entries) gains `adapter`, `uses`, `consumes`,
  `type refined`, `type opaque`, `function with contract`, `actor`,
  `service call handler` (a `given`-bearing `on call`), `suite`, and `property`;
  its existing `agent` snippet is enriched with `invariant`/`transition`.

The starting points were deliberately asymmetric (`bynk.json` already carried
`fn`/`type record`/`type enum`/`provides`/`agent` that `SNIPPETS` lacked;
`SNIPPETS` carried `adapter`/`on call` that `bynk.json` lacked), so the fill adds
each construct to whichever set lacked it rather than forcing one master list.

## Test delta

- `bynk-lsp/tests/scaffolds_compile.rs` (ADR 0157) already parses every entry of
  both catalogues against the current grammar; the new scaffolds are covered
  automatically. Its `support::{classify, wrap_for_parse}` gains the wraps the
  new shapes need: `suite` as a standalone unit, `property`/`case` nested in a
  `suite`, `uses`/`consumes` as unit-header clauses, and `on <method>` handlers
  routed (like `on call`) into a `service` body.
- **New:** `catalogues_cover_the_refreshed_constructs` asserts every construct
  this slice set out to de-stale appears in at least one scaffold across the
  *union* of the two catalogues. This is union coverage, **not** the per-set
  parity DECISION A forbids — only proof the fill happened and can't silently
  regress.

## Decisions

- **DECISION A (settled in the track): two catalogues, not one.** Kept as two
  independent sets checked against the grammar *each*, with no parity/set-equality
  requirement. This slice's coverage test asserts the *union* covers the
  constructs, which does not reintroduce parity — the two sets remain free to
  diverge in what they carry and in their insert-text dialects.
- Supersedes and corrects
  [#307](https://github.com/accuser/bynk/issues/307), whose suggested
  `test "…" { assert … }` scaffold predates the testing track and is itself
  stale; the fill uses the shipped `suite`/`case`/`expect` surface instead.
